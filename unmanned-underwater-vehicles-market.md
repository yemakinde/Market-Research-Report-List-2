<p><p>Unmanned Underwater Vehicles (UUVs) are classified into two main types - Autonomous Underwater Vehicles (AUVs) and Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs). AUVs are autonomous, self-propelled vehicles that are pre-programmed to carry out underwater missions independently, while ROVs are controlled and operated by a human operator from the surface. AUVs are typically used for tasks that require autonomy and endurance, such as deep-sea exploration and underwater mapping, while ROVs are commonly used for tasks that require precise control and manipulation, such as underwater inspections and maintenance.</p></p>
