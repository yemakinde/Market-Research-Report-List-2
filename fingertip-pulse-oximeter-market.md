<p><p>Handheld pulse oximeters are small, portable devices that are held in the hand for spot-checking oxygen saturation levels. These are commonly used in healthcare settings for quick and easy monitoring of patients' oxygen levels. On the other hand, non-linear pulse oximeters are more advanced devices that provide continuous monitoring of oxygen saturation levels over time. These devices are often used in critical care settings where constant monitoring of a patient's oxygen levels is necessary. Both types of pulse oximeters play a crucial role in monitoring and managing patients' respiratory health.</p></p>
