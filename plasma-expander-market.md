<p><p>Plasma expanders are used to increase blood volume in patients with low blood pressure or dehydration. Different types of plasma expanders include Dextran, Hydroxyethyl Starch, Human Albumin, PEGylated Albumin, Polyvinylpyrrolidone (PVP), and Gelatin. Dextran and Hydroxyethyl Starch are synthetic solutions, while Human Albumin is derived from human plasma. PEGylated Albumin is modified for longer circulation in the body. Polyvinylpyrrolidone (PVP) is a polymer-based plasma expander, and Gelatin is derived from animal sources. Each type has its own benefits and applications in clinical settings.</p></p>
